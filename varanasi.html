Tour Itinerary

Day 1

Varanasi railway station

main railway station serving the city of Varanasi train

Kaalbhairav temple

Kaalbhairav Temple in Kashi (Varanasi) is one of the oldest and most revered temples dedicated to Lord Kaalbhairav, a fierce manifestation of Lord Shiva. The temple is considered the guardian deity of the city, and devotees believe that visiting here protects them from negative energies and grants spiritual strength. The temple's unique rituals and powerful atmosphere make it a significant spiritual destination in Varanasi.

Kashi Vishwanath temple

Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the heart of Varanasi. It is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and holds immense spiritual significance. Pilgrims from all over the world visit to seek blessings and experience the vibrant rituals and traditions of this sacred site.

Manikarnika ghat

Manikarnika Ghat is one of the oldest and most sacred ghats in Varanasi, renowned as a major cremation ground. It is believed that those who are cremated here attain moksha (liberation from the cycle of rebirth). The ghat is always bustling with rituals and offers a profound spiritual experience, reflecting the deep-rooted traditions of the city.

Day 2

Varahi temple

Varahi Temple in Varanasi is dedicated to Goddess Varahi, one of the Sapta Matrikas. The temple is revered for its spiritual ambiance and unique rituals. Devotees visit to seek blessings for protection and prosperity, experiencing the traditional architecture and serene environment.

Kashi Karvat

Kashi Karvat is a historic site in Varanasi, known for its unique religious significance. It is believed that those who die here attain salvation. The place is steeped in local legends and attracts visitors interested in the spiritual and cultural heritage of the city.

Evening Ganga aarti

The Evening Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is a mesmerizing spiritual ritual in Varanasi. Priests perform synchronized movements with lamps, incense, and chants, creating a captivating atmosphere along the banks of the Ganges. The ceremony attracts devotees and tourists alike, offering a unique glimpse into the city's vibrant spiritual life.

Day 3

Morning aarti-Assi ghat

The Assi Ghat is one of the most popular ghats in Varanasi, known for its vibrant morning aarti (prayer ceremony) that attracts both locals and tourists. The aarti is a beautiful ritual involving the offering of light to the river Ganges, accompanied by chanting and music.

Boat riding

Boat riding in Kashi (Varanasi) offers a serene and captivating experience along the sacred Ganges River. Glide past historic ghats, witness daily rituals, and soak in the spiritual ambiance of the city. Early morning or evening boat rides provide stunning views of the sunrise, sunset, and the vibrant life along the riverbanks, making it a must-do activity for visitors.
